# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This folder is the centralized entry point for all OctoAcme project management process documentation. Whether you are joining a new project or looking for a refresher on our processes, start here to understand how we plan, execute, and continuously improve our work.

---

## Project Management Process Summary

OctoAcme follows a five-phase project lifecycle:

1. **Initiation** – Define the problem statement, identify stakeholders, and establish a high-level timeline. Produce a Project Charter or one-pager.
2. **Planning** – Finalize scope, resources, milestones, and dependencies. Agree on acceptance criteria and the Definition of Done.
3. **Execution** – Build, test, review, and iterate in short increments. Track progress, manage risks, and maintain clear communication.
4. **Release** – Deploy and verify the solution, then announce the release to stakeholders.
5. **Retrospective & Improvement** – Capture learnings, celebrate wins, and feed action items back into future planning cycles.

---

## Key Principles

- **Customer-first** – Prioritize customer value and usability in every decision.
- **Iterative delivery** – Ship small, testable increments rather than big-bang releases.
- **Clear ownership** – Every project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead who are accountable for outcomes.
- **Data-informed decisions** – Measure impact and iterate based on evidence, not assumptions.
- **Psychological safety** – Encourage open feedback, learning from failure, and continuous improvement.

---

## Core Roles

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| **Project Manager (PM)** | Coordinates delivery, manages schedules, risks, and stakeholder communications. |
| **Product Manager (PdM)** | Defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures product success. |
| **Developers** | Implement features, collaborate on design, write tests, and maintain documentation. |
| **QA / Testing** | Validates quality and acceptance criteria before release. |
| **Stakeholders** | Provide inputs, decisions, and approvals throughout the project lifecycle. |

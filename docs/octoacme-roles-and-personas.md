# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate all aspects of the release process, ensuring all artifacts are verified and approved before deployment. They serve as the central point of communication for release status, issues, and go/no-go decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases in coordination with project and product teams
- Verify that all release artifacts, tests, and approvals are complete before deployment
- Coordinate deployment activities across development, QA, and operations teams
- Communicate release status, blockers, and outcomes to stakeholders
- Maintain release notes, deployment checklists, and rollback plans

### Goals
- Ensure releases are delivered on schedule with minimal risk
- Maintain a reliable, repeatable release process
- Minimize production incidents caused by deployment issues

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reports and go/no-go decisions
- Deployment runbooks and rollback procedures
- Post-release summaries and incident retrospectives

### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Aligns release schedule with project timelines and milestones
- **QA**: Confirms test completion and sign-off before deployment
- **Developers**: Coordinates code freeze, artifact builds, and hotfix procedures
- **Product Lead**: Obtains final approval and communicates release outcomes

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove obstacles for the delivery team, and reinforce Agile best practices. They serve as a coach and servant leader, helping the team continuously improve its processes and effectiveness.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove impediments that block team progress
- Shield the team from external interruptions and scope creep
- Coach team members on Agile principles and practices
- Track team velocity and support continuous process improvement

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver consistently and sustainably
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety
- Ensure Agile ceremonies are productive and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremony facilitation and meeting notes
- Impediment logs and escalation summaries
- Team health and velocity reports

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Removes blockers and supports self-organization within the team
- **Project Manager**: Aligns sprint goals with project milestones and schedules
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog is refined and sprint goals are well-defined

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide deep domain-specific knowledge to inform technical and integration decisions. They review solution approaches and serve as a reference for complex functional or regulatory requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Provide expert guidance on domain-specific requirements and constraints
- Review and validate solution designs, technical approaches, and acceptance criteria
- Participate in requirement workshops and design reviews
- Identify risks related to domain compliance, integration, or usability
- Support QA in defining test cases for domain-specific scenarios

### Goals
- Ensure solutions are technically sound and fit for purpose within the domain
- Reduce rework caused by domain knowledge gaps
- Enable informed decision-making across cross-functional teams

### Typical Communication
- Design and requirements review feedback
- Domain knowledge documentation and reference materials
- Ad-hoc consultation during planning and development

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Provides domain context to guide implementation and resolve ambiguity
- **Product Manager**: Informs feature prioritization and acceptance criteria with domain expertise
- **Stakeholders**: Bridges the gap between business needs and technical feasibility

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers own user research, define user flows, and create design assets that guide the development of intuitive, user-centered products. They ensure the team has the design context needed to deliver a high-quality user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings into actionable insights
- Define user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity design assets
- Collaborate with Product Managers to align design with product goals
- Work with Developers to ensure accurate implementation of designs
- Support QA in validating that the delivered experience matches design intent

### Goals
- Deliver user experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and valuable
- Ensure design assets and research are available before development begins
- Reduce usability issues discovered late in the project lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Design specs, prototypes, and annotated wireframes
- Usability research reports and user journey maps
- Design review sessions and feedback documentation

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on product vision, user needs, and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Provides design assets, clarifies intent, and reviews implementation
- **QA**: Reviews delivered UI against design specs and usability standards

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather requirements, document business needs, and synthesize findings into user stories and acceptance criteria. They serve as a bridge between stakeholders and the delivery team to ensure shared understanding of project goals.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business and functional requirements
- Translate stakeholder needs into well-defined user stories and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirement workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Maintain a requirements traceability matrix to track coverage and completeness
- Identify gaps, ambiguities, and conflicts in requirements and facilitate resolution

### Goals
- Ensure the delivery team has clear, complete, and actionable requirements
- Minimize scope creep and requirement-related rework
- Maintain alignment between business goals and delivered solutions

### Typical Communication
- User stories and acceptance criteria in the project backlog
- Requirements documentation and business process models
- Workshop facilitation notes and stakeholder sign-off records

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates to refine the backlog and align requirements with product strategy
- **Project Manager**: Supports planning by providing well-scoped requirements and effort estimates
- **Stakeholders**: Engages directly to capture needs, validate understanding, and obtain approvals
